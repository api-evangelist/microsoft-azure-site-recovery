<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="why-this-recognition-matters">Why this recognition matters</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Forrester’s research highlights a key reality of sovereign clouds: there is no single deployment model that fits every sovereignty requirement. Instead, organizations combine public cloud, private cloud, and disconnected environments to achieve the level of sovereignty they need – balancing risk, regulations, functionality, and cost.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">In this context, leadership isn’t about offering a single “sovereign cloud.” The goal is not isolation, but it’s about providing consistent sovereign controls across multiple environments to maintain access to modern cloud capabilities.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Forrester places Microsoft in the Leaders category based on its scores in the current offering and strategy categories. The report also notes Microsoft&#8217;s vision to offer sovereign controls across cloud, AI and productivity services. Specifically, <a href="https://info.microsoft.com/ww-landing-microsoft-sovereign-cloud-in-europe.html" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Microsoft’s ability to extend sovereignty across AI, productivity, security, and cloud platform</a>.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="a-platform-approach-to-sovereignty">A platform approach to sovereignty</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><a href="https://reprint.forrester.com/reports/the-forrester-wave-tm-sovereign-cloud-platforms-q2-2026-f64f29c7/index.html" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">The Forrester report</a> notes that Microsoft&#8217;s sovereign capabilities are available consistently for both private and public cloud. In practice, digital sovereignty is achieved through a combination of technical controls, operational practices, and contractual commitments applied consistently across deployment models.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/sovereignty">Microsoft Sovereign Cloud</a> brings together:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Public cloud with data residency and access controls, including region-specific residency controls such as EU Data Boundary.</li>



<li class="wp-block-list-item">Private cloud with hybrid deployments, enabled through <a href="https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/products/local/">Azure Local</a> and consistent policy and management via <a href="https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/products/azure-arc/">Azure Arc</a>.</li>



<li class="wp-block-list-item">Partner-operated national clouds, with Bleu and Delos Cloud, where infrastructure is independently owned and operated to meet national requirements.</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This approach allows organizations to grow their sovereign IT posture over time, adapting to evolving regulatory, operational, or geopolitical conditions without having to abandon the Microsoft cloud ecosystem.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="consistency-across-sovereign-environments">Consistency across sovereign environments</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">One of the differentiators cited in the evaluation is Microsoft’s ability to make key capabilities available across sovereign public and sovereign private cloud. Forrester specifically calls out Microsoft’s container and Kubernetes capabilities, including the use of Azure Arc and Azure Local to run Kubernetes clusters in connected or disconnected environments, supported by infrastructure-as-code and GitOps tooling.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This consistency matters because sovereign cloud isn’t just about where data resides, but about whether organizations can:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Operate and secure workloads the same way across environments.</li>



<li class="wp-block-list-item">Maintain development and operation standards.</li>



<li class="wp-block-list-item">Avoid fragmenting teams, tools, and processes.</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">By extending common management, governance, and deployment models across environments, <a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/sovereignty" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Microsoft Sovereign Cloud</a> helps reduce complexity while giving organizations control.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="looking-ahead">Looking ahead</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Sovereign cloud platforms are evolving quickly, especially as customers look to apply AI, analytics, and modern application services across different environments. Forrester notes that customers don’t “buy” sovereignty as a standalone product, they architect for it over time.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Microsoft’s recognition as a Leader in this evaluation underscores our commitment to keep investing in sovereign cloud innovation such as:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li class="wp-block-list-item">Advanced AI development and runtime capabilities.</li>



<li class="wp-block-list-item">Increasing consistency and parity across deployment models.</li>



<li class="wp-block-list-item">Supporting customers as sovereignty requirements continue to mature and evolve.</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We’re grateful to our customers and partners who continue to shape our approach and we remain focused on helping organizations adopt cloud and AI with confidence, flexibility, and transparency wherever their workloads need to run.</p>
